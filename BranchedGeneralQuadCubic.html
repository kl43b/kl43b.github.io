Prerequisite

You should understand plain old antiderivatives and derivatives for this text, i.e.:

  • \(\frac{d}{dx}\left(x^{3}+x^{2}\right)=3x^{2}+2x\)
  • \(\int_{ }^{ }\left(x^{2}+x\right)dx=\frac{x^{3}}{3}+\frac{x^{2}}{2}\)

Generalizing root-extraction for higher degrees

You may have noticed that the quadratic-root and cubic-extrema equations differ by only two numbers:

\[x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-{\color{orange}4}ac} \over {\color{orange}2}a}\]      \[x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-{\color{orange}3}ac} \over {\color{orange}3}a}\]
...but you may not know that it can be extended to the quartic, quintic and beyond:

\[x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-{\color{orange}{8 \over 3}}ac} \over {\color{orange}4}a}\]      \[x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-{\color{orange}{5 \over 2}}ac} \over {\color{orange}5}a}\]
Generalizing these equations is actually easily accomplished by remembering that all the coefficients are associated with a degree'd term.

Open the next section to see how to generalize the quadratic.

The General Quadratic

We need to keep track of the degree associated with each coefficient.

For example, \(a\) is associated with \(x^2\). Let's just drop \(x\) and track its exponent on the coefficient -- with \({\color{orange}\left\{n\right\}}\) to help us remember it's not part of the equation:

\(a^{\color{orange}{\left\{2\right\}}}\) \(b^{\color{orange}{\left\{1\right\}}}\) \(c^{\color{orange}{\left\{0\right\}}}\) then... \[x = {-b^{\color{orange}\left\{1\right\}} \pm \sqrt{(b^{\color{orange}\left\{1\right\}})^2-4a^{\color{orange}\left\{2\right\}}c^{\color{orange}\left\{0\right\}}} \over 2a^{\color{orange}\left\{2\right\}}}\]

...and then we integrate the coefficients inside the quadratic equation:

\(\frac{-\frac{b^{\color{orange}{\left\{2\right\}}}}{2}+\sqrt{\left(\frac{b^{\color{orange}{\left\{2\right\}}}}{2}\right)^{2}-4\left(\frac{a^{\color{orange}{\left\{3\right\}}}}{3}\right)\left(\frac{c^{\color{orange}{\left\{1\right\}}}}{1}\right)}}{2\left(\frac{a^{\color{orange}{\left\{3\right\}}}}{3}\right)}\)

We're only doing one integration (quadratic to cubic) so we can drop our tracked values.

\(\frac{-\frac{b}{2}+\sqrt{\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^{2}-4\left(\frac{a}{3}\right)\left(\frac{c}{1}\right)}}{2\left(\frac{a}{3}\right)}\)
